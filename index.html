About Me

Hello World! Just kidding. I'm about as tired seeing that phrase as I am writing it. Seriously though welcome to my online portfolio. If you can't already tell my personality is about as colorful as this site. I'm from Houston, Texas which is where I currently live. I have 3 children who take up a lot of my time but when I'm not laying the Smack Down on them I enjoy going to art shows, cooking or just plain nerding out on documentaries. I love to learn new things!

Right now I am working in the oil and gas industry as a mechanical designer creating downhole tools but I am looking to make a career change into web development. I love working with people on projects and I'm not afraid of a challenge. I have gone through numerous online tutorials and online courses including Udacity's Grow With Google Front End Web Development program. Two of my favorite developers that I follow and have taken online courses from are Wes Bos and Brad Traversy.

The Adventures of Little Tikka

This is one of the first projects that I created because I saw a website that used parallax scrolling to tell a beautiful story Jess and Russ and it really inspired me. So I created my own version to tell the story of my youngest son who goes by the nickname "Tikka". Think Look Who's Talking meets James Bond. This is a work in progress that I try and add to whenever I have spare time.
Since this is a parallax site it does not work on mobile.

Memory Game

I created a memory game that was part of the curriculum at Udacity. I used vanilla Javascript. My youngest son loves playing this game which is pretty cool. I plan on having different levels and creating different cards to match (words) and add in audio for each card to help him increase his vocabulary.

US Timezones

I originally saw this tutorial as part of Wes Bos's Javascript 30 course and thought why show one clock when you can show 4? So I created a timezone site that shows you the timezones in the United States no matter what time zone the user is in. I used vanilla Javascript as well as the Moment library.

Restaurant Picker

At my job my co-workers and I are the most indecisive group of people when it comes to choosing a place to eat at lunch so I decided to create this simple Python program so that we could choose a random restaurant to eat at. There is no GUI but you can view, randomize, add or delete a restaurant.
